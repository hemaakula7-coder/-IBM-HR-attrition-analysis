# 🏢 HR Attrition Analysis

## 📌 Overview
Analysis of IBM HR dataset of 1,470 employees to identify 
why employees leave and key patterns driving attrition.

## 📂 Dataset
- **Source:** IBM HR Analytics Dataset (Kaggle)
- **Size:** 1,470 employees | 32 features
- **Target Variable:** Attrition (Yes/No)

## 📊 Visualizations
| Chart | Key Insight |
|---|---|
| Attrition by Department | Sales highest at 20.6% |
| Age Distribution | 26–35 age group most at risk |
| Monthly Income vs Attrition | Lower salary = higher attrition |
| Correlation Heatmap | JobLevel & Income are key drivers |
| Attrition by Job Role | Sales Rep at ~40% — critically high |

## 🔍 Key Findings
- 📌 **Sales Representatives** have highest attrition at ~40%
- 📌 **Employees aged 26–35** are most likely to leave
- 📌 **Lower monthly income** is strongly linked to attrition
- 📌 **Senior roles** (Manager, Research Director) are most stable
- 📌 **JobLevel & MonthlyIncome** are the strongest retention factors

## 💡 Business Recommendations
1. Review compensation for Sales & entry-level roles
2. Build clear career growth paths for high-risk roles
3. Reduce mandatory overtime to improve work-life balance
4. Introduce retention bonuses for the 26–35 age group
5. Conduct stay interviews before employees decide to leave
